# Executive Summary

The North America region closed Q1 2026 at 94% of quota, with total revenue of $4.7M against a target of $5.0M. New business acquisition was strong, while expansion revenue from existing accounts came in below plan due to delayed renewals in the enterprise segment.

Key results:

- New business revenue: **$2.9M** (108% of target)
- Expansion revenue: **$1.8M** (79% of target)
- Average deal size increased by **18%** year-over-year
- Sales cycle length reduced from 47 days to 38 days

# Background

## Regional Context

The North America team entered Q1 2026 with a restructured territory model following the addition of three new account executives in Q4 2025. Ramp periods for new hires affected capacity in January and February.

## Q4 2025 Carryover

Three enterprise deals totaling $620K that were forecast to close in Q4 2025 slipped into Q1. Two of these closed in January; the third remains in late-stage negotiation.

# Methodology

## Data Sources

Performance data was pulled from Salesforce on March 24, 2026. Pipeline figures reflect opportunities with a close date in Q1 2026 and a stage of Proposal or later as of January 1.

## Metrics Definitions

| Metric       | Definition                                  |
| ------------ | ------------------------------------------- |
| New Business | First-time contracts with net-new logos     |
| Expansion    | Upsells and renewals with existing accounts |
| Win Rate     | Closed-won / (Closed-won + Closed-lost)     |
| Sales Cycle  | Days from opportunity creation to close     |

# Findings

## Revenue Attainment

New business outperformed due to strong demand in the mid-market segment. The enterprise segment underperformed on expansion, primarily driven by two large renewals pushed to Q2 by procurement delays at the client side.

## Win/Loss Analysis

| Segment    | Win Rate | Change vs Q4 2025 |
| ---------- | -------- | ----------------- |
| SMB        | 38%      | +4 pts            |
| Mid-Market | 44%      | +7 pts            |
| Enterprise | 29%      | −3 pts            |

Enterprise win rate declined due to increased competition from two new market entrants. Loss interviews cited pricing flexibility and integration depth as the primary decision factors.

## Pipeline Health

End-of-quarter pipeline coverage stands at 3.2× for Q2, above the 3.0× target. However, 40% of the pipeline is concentrated in 8 accounts, representing meaningful concentration risk.

# Recommendations

Based on Q1 findings, the following actions are recommended for Q2 2026:

1. Prioritize enterprise renewal risk: assign dedicated customer success support to the 5 at-risk accounts flagged in the loss analysis
2. Address pricing flexibility gap: work with product and finance to define a tiered pricing structure for enterprise deals above $200K
3. Reduce pipeline concentration: set a target that no single account represents more than 8% of quarterly pipeline

# Conclusion

Q1 2026 demonstrated strong new business momentum and improving sales efficiency. Closing the expansion gap and addressing competitive positioning in enterprise will be the primary focus areas for Q2.
